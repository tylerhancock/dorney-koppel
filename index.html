Pennies per Person?
"People Need to Wake Up."

30 million Americans have COPD and 12-15 million don't even know it. Last year alone, 165,000 people died from COPD.

30 Million are Suffering

Our country allocates mere pennies per person on COPD research each year. Pennies, to fight a disease that kills more Americans every year than the last 4 wars combined. That's the message we're sending to people struggling with COPD.

Most people know Chronic Pulmonary Obstructive Disease (COPD) by other names â€“ like emphysema or chronic bronchitis. It's a chronic lung condition that gets worse with time, and makes those that suffer from it feel like they can never catch their breath.

Why is the #3 Killer 165th in Funding?

Right now, someone you know has COPD. So why is the #3 killer among chronic diseases just 165th in funding? With a greater focus on treatments, respiratory therapies, education and, ultimately, a cure, we can create hope for people living with this disease. That's why the MoreForCOPD initiative exists. Our Story

"Neglected, Not Taken Care of By My Own Government."

We all know someone with COPD. They're our mothers, fathers, friends, and neighbors. Our truck drivers, teachers, athletes, and artists. They're from small towns in West Virginia and big cities like New York. They're people, Americans, just like us.

Some got it from smoking. Others from where they worked or lived. And some never touched a cigarette a day in their life. But they all share one thing: they're fighting this devastating disease while fighting for air.

Their lives are worth more than pennies. These are their stories.
