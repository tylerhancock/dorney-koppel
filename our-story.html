The Dorney-Koppel Foundation

Ever since her diagnosis, Grace Anne Dorney Koppel has been a voice for those with COPD. She and her husband, Ted Koppel, created the Dorney-Koppel Foundation to bring attention to this devastating disease and lead the charge to fight it.

Those with COPD are limited to a fraction of a healthy person's lung capacity. This can make it hard or impossible to play sports, walk at the same pace as others, or even work a job. The Dorney-Koppel Foundation has helped cofound clinics thhroughout the country that focus on pulmonary rehabilitation (breathing practices that help retain what lung capacity is still present). Now,

Grace Anne and Ted have set their sights on Congress, demanding that COPD sufferers get the funding they deserve in pursuit of a cure.

copd: the disease

The #3 killer among chronic diseases in America affects 30 million of us. Here is some information that can help those who may be affected.

How do you get copd?

There are many different ways to get COPD. Smoking cigarettes, breathing in secondhand smoke, and breathing in chemical fumes, dust, or toxic substances can all cause the disease. In fact, 20-30% of people with COPD can trace their disease to workplace hazards.

what are the symptoms of copd?

People with COPD often experience a chronic cough, wheezing, or a feeling that they're out of breath more frequently during activities that were once easy.

If you thihnk you may have COPD, talk to your doctor about receiving a diagnostic pulmonary lung function test.

What resources are out there?

Though we're out to get more funding for research, there are some helpful resources for those with COPD.

The COPD Foundation: The COPD Foundation was established to expand services for COPD and improve the lives of individuals affected by the disease. The foundation focuses on achieving these results through research, education and advocacy programs that will lead to prevention, and someday, a cure for this disease. Learn More
